## 2. Research and Brainstorming

### Research Process

The team conducted both individual and group research to identify real-world problems addressable through digital solutions. Research sources included:

- Local community challenges and observations of daily-life inefficiencies
- Market trends in digital platforms and mobile services
- Startup case studies from regional and global markets
- Personal experiences and gaps identified within the local ecosystem

### Brainstorming Techniques Used

To maximize idea diversity and quality, the team applied three structured brainstorming methods:

- **Mind Mapping:** Used to visualize connections between identified problems and potential solutions, helping the team see patterns across different domains.
- **SCAMPER Framework:** Applied to challenge and innovate on existing ideas by asking: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to another use, Eliminate, and Reverse.
- **"How Might We" Questions:** Used to reframe problems as opportunities and encourage open-ended, creative thinking (e.g., *"How might we help residents find trusted local services faster?"*).

### Ideas Generated

The brainstorming session produced the following five candidate ideas:

## 3. Idea Evaluation

### Evaluation Criteria

Each idea was assessed using a structured scoring rubric based on four key criteria:

| Criterion | Description |
|---|---|
| **Feasibility** | Can the MVP be realistically built within the team's technical capabilities and timeline? |
| **Social Impact** | Does the idea address a genuine problem and benefit a meaningful user group? |
| **Market Demand** | Is there a clear and existing demand for this solution? |
| **Technical Complexity** | Is the required technology manageable for the team at this stage? |

### Evaluation Summary

#### SafeRide – Secure Ride-Sharing Platform
- **Strengths:** High social impact, strong and proven market demand
- **Weaknesses:** Significant regulatory and legal challenges; highly competitive market with established players
- **Verdict:** Rejected — high barriers to entry make MVP development impractical within the project scope

#### ArabicBook – Arabic Digital Publishing Platform
- **Strengths:** Addresses a real gap in the Arabic digital content ecosystem; culturally significant
- **Weaknesses:** Unclear and uncertain monetization model; slow content ecosystem to grow
- **Verdict:** Rejected — long-term viability concerns and unclear revenue path

#### RealChain – Real Estate Supply Chain Management Platform
- **Strengths:** High enterprise value; scalable solution with strong B2B potential
- **Weaknesses:** Requires extensive industry partnerships that are difficult to secure quickly
- **Verdict:** Rejected — dependency on external partnerships creates high risk for early-stage development

#### Hayyak – Smart Neighborhood Services Platform
- **Strengths:** Strong local social impact; achievable MVP scope; clear and identified market gap; community-oriented
- **Weaknesses:** Requires buy-in from local businesses and service providers for adoption
- **Verdict:** **Selected** — best overall balance of feasibility, impact, and scalability

#### CarLeaseHub – Car Rental Brokerage Platform
- **Strengths:** Existing and demonstrated market demand
- **Weaknesses:** Highly competitive space; difficult to differentiate without significant resources
- **Verdict:** Rejected — insufficient competitive advantage at the MVP stage

### Final Scoring Result

After applying the structured rubric and discussing results as a team, **Hayyak** achieved the highest overall score, demonstrating the strongest balance across all four evaluation dimensions.

## 4. Decision and Refinement

### Selected MVP: Hayyak – Smart Neighborhood Services Platform

#### Problem Statement

Residents frequently struggle to find trusted, nearby service providers within their neighborhoods. Current digital platforms focus on city-wide or national scale, leaving a clear gap in hyper-local service ecosystems. This results in wasted time, reliance on word-of-mouth, and difficulty for small local businesses to reach nearby customers.

#### Target Users

| User Group | Description |
|---|---|
| Neighborhood Residents | Individuals seeking quick, trusted access to local services |
| Local Service Providers | Grocery stores, laundries, technicians, and other small businesses |

#### Key Features

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| Local Services Directory | A searchable, categorized listing of verified neighborhood service providers |
| Service Request System | Allows residents to submit and track service requests directly through the platform |
| Resident-Provider Communication | In-app messaging enabling direct, transparent communication |
| Digital Payments Integration | Secure, seamless payment processing within the platform |
| Neighborhood Announcements | A community board for local updates, events, and notices |

#### Reasons for Selection

- **Feasibility:** The MVP can be built using standard web/mobile technologies within the project timeline.
- **Innovation:** Hayyak introduces a hyper-local focus not addressed by existing mainstream platforms.
- **Alignment with Goals:** The idea aligns with the team's technical skills and interest in community-driven solutions.
- **Social Value:** It directly benefits both residents and small local businesses, driving real-world impact.

#### Potential Challenges and Opportunities

| Challenges | Opportunities |
|---|---|
| Onboarding local service providers to the platform | Partnering with local municipalities and community centers for distribution |
| Building trust and credibility in a new platform | Rapid growth potential by expanding to additional neighborhoods |
| Ensuring data privacy and secure transactions | Revenue through premium listings, featured promotions, and transaction fees |
